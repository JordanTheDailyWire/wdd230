Hick's Law

Amazon

Amazon

I would say Amazon fits into a hick's law. Where the user is given more options to chosen from which makes the user take longer to decide on that item. The more the user clicks on an item to view the more options is given to the user through that item. It will take the user a while to shop on Amazon because they want to find the best affordable product on Amazon. As more products are being released, the more that Amazon will increase there options.

Fitt's Law

Redbox

Redbox

I felt that Redbox fits into the fitt's law. The way the it has it's target element closer then streaming platforms. The shopping cart is on the right and above that you have the sign in. As well as the search bar, this is a good example of what fitt's law would look like because I would not have to spend time looking for the shopping or sign in button, they are already there. Redbox gives the user everything he would need to use in there website, giving the user to use the website as quickly as possible.

Rule of Thirds

Peony and Pine

Peony and Pine

Peony and Pine fits into the rule of thirds. The rule of thirds is have images or a video used throughout the website in a unique way without centering every image. Peony and pine photography shows there photography in a interesting way. One section of the homepage you get a stack of photos that are together and as you go through the webpage you get all sorts of landscapes and wedding photos that are sperade and it gives the user an idea of what the company is all about without have to search or find examples in the website. It is all displayed on that webpage.
